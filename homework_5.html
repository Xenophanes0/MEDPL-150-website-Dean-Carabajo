I wanted to create something in illustrator that further reinfroced the pollution theme of the original photomontage that it is being attached to, so I decided to base my illsutrator document off of the premise of the film "The Host" (2006), by Bong Joon-ho, and it's surrounding backstory to the film. The film is about a sea creature emerging from the Han River and kipnapping a man's daughter, with the man and his extended family's attempts to rescue the daughter. An important plot plot to the film being how unsympathetic government officials are in the film, as the creation of the monster in the film was due to an American military pathologist demanding for an Korean assistant to violate protocols and simpliy dump over 200 bottles worthy of formaldehyde down a drain, which led into the Han River. If exposed to the chemical long term, it is capable of producing cancer into animals, inlcuding people, while in the film it caused a giant monster that feeds off of people. However the director of the film did note that he based the film off of an article coming out in regards of a deformed fish with an S-shaped spine caght in the Han River. This aritcle likely refurs to an incident that occurred in Feburary 2000, where an American mortician, working for the U.S> military, had forced a subordinate to dump over 480 bottles of formaldehyde down a drain, which led to the Han River, which the U.S. miltary later had to aplogize for. Considering the timeline of events, they are most likkely correlated to one another. Anyways, with my previous reasons of creating my photmontage centering around pollution, and The Host's commentary of pollution harming the environment with the real-life incident involving the American miltiary, I wanted to based my illustration in a similar vein as how the monster from The Host came about. So I drew in a monster whoose entire being centered around vast amounts of garbage, being the first boss of Yo Kai Watch 3: Junkernaut. He is espically a spirit that ressides in a scrapyard and uses the surrounding scrap as armor through assimilation. He is even encountered thorugh the main cast of the game investating rumors of a "monster in the scrap yard" and battle him when he makes himself known. Figured with how I framed my photograph with what each image represents and the image as a whole, Junkernaut would have made for a fine addition to the photomontage, with the people still choosing to ignore the issue of pollution, even when issues in nature start to arise, like the one deformed fish. Just have Junkernaut be the replacement in this case.

I was able to create my illsutration through the use of the shapes tool to construct the body, eye and various part's of the armor Junkernaut is wearing. I used the drawing and pen tools to create portions of Junkernaut that are more fluid, to not make it look rigid if I used shapes, like the arms, fan baldes on his left hand and the claw portions of his right hand. I did my best with trying to recreate Junkernaut with illsuutrator tools and still have him be recongizable enough to where someone who play Yo Kai Watch 3 could see the resemblance, without every small detail being there.
